  • What is this game?
    • FrogComposBand is a roguelike game made from variants, of variants of Angband.
  • What is a Roguelike?
    • In 1980 a video game was released for personal home computers; Rogue (Rogue: Exploring the Dungeons of Doom)
    • This video game was revolutionary as it used procedural generation to provide the player with a near limitless amount of content. Levels would never be the same, the items you come in contact with had randomized stats, and the video game had an unforgiving difficulty
    • In Rogue dying was as much part of the game as playing it in the first place. You (the user) learn, while your adventurers die.
    • Today we use the term Roguelike to describe games that are - like Rogue. The more like Rogue a game is, the more of a roguelike it is considered. Other elements of procedural generation exist, and some games offer meta progression. These games are generally considered Rogue-lite games.
    • Rogue (Wikipedia)
  • What is Angband?
    • In 1990 a Roguelike was released for personal home computers named Angband- it was a roguelike set in the fantasy world of Middle Earth, a product of the writer J.R.R. Tolkien
    • In this game players could come in contact with famous Middle Earth characters, such as Gollum. It had a starter town, and a nearly limitless dungeon. The player was encouraged to level, progress, and make it to the bottom of the dungeon of Angband and defeat Morgoth.
    • While Angband was popular in its own right; it represented another revolution in Roguelikes- games that are variants of Angbands are generally called 'bands'. Multiple variants popped up, ZAngband, MAngband, and many more!
    • These variants would, themselves, become the basis of other variants - from ZAngband came Cthangband, PernAngband, and Hengband.
